GATTE KI SABZI

Most of Rajasthani cusine came to fruition as a result of the
arid conditions. For this dish you don’t need any fresh vegetables and that’s
its speciality. This curry is made with gram flour dumplings (steamed and lightly fried) and
tangy gravy made up of tomato, buttermilk and spices. It’s best enjoyed
with rotis (Indian flat bread) and rice.





DALBATI CHURMA

This famous Rajasthani dish needs no introduction.
This is the state's classic signature dish. Dal bati >churma is synonymous to Rajasthan;
known for its crunchy batis, dipped in ghee along with
spicy daal and sweet churma..





GHEVAR

Ghevar or Ghewar is a Rajasthani cuisine disc-shaped sweet made
from ghee, flour, and sugar syrup. It is traditionally associated with the month of Shravan
and the Teej and Raksha Bandhan festivals.





MALPUA

In the Jagannath temple, Puri, malpua is offered as the first
thing (Sakala Dhupa) to Lord Jagannath in the morning every day. Popularly known
as 'Amalu', this sweet dish is also an integral part of the Chappan Bhog,
s offered to Lord Jagannath.





RABARI

Rabdi, also called rabri, is a classic North Indian treat
that's rich, creamy, and perfectly sweet. To make rabri recipe, milk is cooked until it reduces
to a thick pudding-like consistency, then is flavored with cardamoms, saffron, and
your favorite nuts and dried fruits..





LAPSI

Lapsi is a traditional recipe from the Marwad region of Rajasthan
and is made during special occasions and fasting days. A straightforward recipe
made with broken wheat also known as bulgur wheat or Dalia,
jaggery, ghee, and water..





KADHI-PAKODA

It is widely believed that the kadhi originated in the deserts of
Rajasthan, where cooks started using dairy products to supplement scarce vegetables. “Some also
believe that the yogurt-based Karhi is from Northwestern India and is a precursor to what came to be
known as the British curry,” writes food historian KT Achaya.





KACHORI

Pyaz ki kachori probably originated in Jaipur, Jodhpur
but are today popular throughout Rajasthan. Very few households actually prepare these crisp,
flaky deep-fried pyaz ki kachori stuffed with an onion filling. Steaming hot Pyaaz ki Kachoris
or Aloo Pyaaz ki Kachoris are sold at most 'namkeen' shops in Rajasthan.





MIRACHI-VADA

The mirchi bada of Jodhpur, Rajasthan is famous, as the water
in that region lends it a unique taste. It combines well with another Jodhpur
speciality, mawa kachori, dipped in sugary syrup..





PANCHKUTA

This is a quintessential curry made up of five ingredients
found widely across the Thar Desert. For a long time, travellers in the desert relied extensively
on panchkuta during long drives on carts and camels. It has a long shelf life after being
cooked and is traditionally eaten with pooris/rotis. The five ingredients of panchkuta are
sangri, ker, Kumat (seeds from the pod of a deciduous tree), gunda (a kind of wild berry) and dry red chillies.





BUNDI-RAYATA

In Rajasthan, people need plenty of water to avoid
dehydration, But the whole of Rajasthan is covered with the desert this is why there is a
lot of water shortage here. People barely get water to get them hydrated. There is a dish in
Rajasthan that removes this problem, and this food is Boondi Raita...





BALUSHAHI

BALUSHAHI is another famous Rajasthani dessert. Balushahi
is like a donut in shape which is prepared during Indian weddings and festivities. This
delicious dessert is prepared with all purpose flour, desi ghee, yogurt, and sugar. This Indian
donut shaped dessert is deep fried and dipped in sugar syrup.





BHUJIYA

Bikaneri bhujia, often simply called bhujia, is a popular
crispy snack prepared by using moth bean flour and gram flour and spices, originating from
Bikaner, a city in the western state of Rajasthan in India. It is
light yellow in colour..





BAJARE-KI-ROTI-WITH-LASUN-CHATANI

Bajra is black millet flour and enjoyed all over the
state. In villages, thickly rolled bajra rotis are cooked over cow dung cakes that impart a smoked
flavour to the rotis. Bajra rotis can accompany virtually any vegetable on a Rajasthani
menu. Bajra roti is usually accompanied by lasun ki chutney- a garlic dip made from garlic, red
chilli powder, lime juice, jaggery and homemade butter.





RAJASTHANI-RAAB

Bajre Ki Khatti Raabdi/Raab is a traditional Rajasthani drink which
has been an important part of daily diet of people all over Rajasthan from past many centuries.
It is usually served along with the meals.Bajra/Pearl Millet is highly rich in essential compounds
like protein, fibre, phosphorus, magnesium and iron. Due to its rich compositionof minerals
and proteins, it has numerous health benefits such as powerful for controlling
diabetes, weight loss, reduces cholesterol, helps in digestion et al.Serve Rajasthani
Bajre Ki Khatti Raabdi with Bajre Ki Roti and Ker-Sangri Ka Saar for a wholesome
weekday lunch.





KER-SANGARI

Rajasthani Ker Sangri Achaar Is A Very Unique And Popular Pickle
From Rajasthani Cuisine. It Is A Flavorful Dry Mix Of Ker And Sangri Pickled In Indian
Spices. These Unique Varieties Of Berries And Beans ( Ker And Sangari ) Which Grows Only In Arid
Deserts Of Rajasthan. Ker Sangri Pickle Is Quite Spicy And Tangy.
